ROLLING MILL MOTOR - MAINTENANCE MANUAL
ABB Motor Type: M3BP 315 SMB 4
Rated Power: 250 kW | Speed: 1485 RPM | Voltage: 415V

=======================================================
SECTION 1: NORMAL OPERATING PARAMETERS
=======================================================
- Temperature (winding): Max 120°C, Normal 60-80°C
- Vibration: Max 2.8 mm/s, Normal 0.5-1.5 mm/s
- Current: Max 32A, Normal 18-22A
- Pressure (cooling air): 68-75 bar
- RPM: 1400-1600 RPM normal range

=======================================================
SECTION 2: BEARING MAINTENANCE
=======================================================
Bearing Type: Deep Groove Ball Bearing 6205-2Z
Lubrication: Shell Alvania EP2 grease
Regreasing interval: Every 2000 operating hours
Bearing replacement interval: Every 20,000 hours or on failure

BEARING REPLACEMENT PROCEDURE:
Step 1: Isolate power - Apply Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)
Step 2: Allow motor to cool to below 40°C before touching
Step 3: Remove motor end covers using 24mm socket
Step 4: Use bearing puller SKF TMMP 5 to extract old bearing
Step 5: Clean shaft and housing with solvent
Step 6: Inspect shaft for scoring - replace if diameter reduced >0.1mm
Step 7: Heat new bearing to 80°C using bearing heater
Step 8: Press new bearing onto shaft - ensure it seats fully
Step 9: Apply 15g of Shell Alvania EP2 grease to new bearing
Step 10: Reinstall end covers - torque bolts to 45 Nm
Step 11: Rotate shaft by hand - should turn freely
Step 12: Run motor unloaded for 30 minutes and monitor temperature

SPARE PARTS FOR BEARING REPLACEMENT:
- Bearing 6205-2Z (Part No: B6205) x2 - drive end and non-drive end
- Bearing seal (Part No: BS-001) x2
- Grease Shell Alvania EP2 - 500g
- End cover gasket (Part No: ECG-001) x2

=======================================================
SECTION 3: COOLING SYSTEM MAINTENANCE
=======================================================
- Clean external cooling fins every 500 hours
- Check cooling fan blade for cracks every 1000 hours
- Replace cooling fan (Part No: CFB-150) if cracked or imbalanced
- Ensure ventilation gap of minimum 200mm around motor

OVERHEATING CAUSES AND REMEDIES:
1. Blocked cooling fins -> Clean with compressed air
2. Cooling fan damaged -> Replace Part No CFB-150
3. Excessive ambient temperature -> Improve room ventilation
4. Overloaded motor -> Reduce load or upgrade motor
5. Blocked ventilation inlet -> Clear obstruction

=======================================================
SECTION 4: VIBRATION DIAGNOSIS
=======================================================
Vibration > 2.8 mm/s: WARNING - Inspect immediately
Vibration > 4.0 mm/s: CRITICAL - Shut down immediately

VIBRATION CAUSES:
- Bearing wear: High broadband vibration, elevated temperature
- Shaft misalignment: 2x frequency peak, axial vibration
- Imbalance: 1x frequency dominant
- Loose mounting: Multiple harmonics
- Gear wear: High frequency spikes

VIBRATION TROUBLESHOOTING:
1. Measure at bearing housing - both ends
2. Compare drive end vs non-drive end
3. High at drive end only -> coupling or alignment
4. High at both ends -> bearing wear or imbalance
5. Use laser alignment tool if misalignment suspected

=======================================================
SECTION 5: ELECTRICAL MAINTENANCE
=======================================================
- Insulation resistance check: Every 6 months, min 1 MΩ
- Winding resistance check: Every 12 months
- Thermal protection check: Every 6 months
- Replace thermal protector (Part No: TP-25) if tripping falsely

ELECTRICAL FAULT SYMPTOMS:
- High current + normal vibration = Electrical issue
- Single phase operation = Check contactor (Part No: CT-25A)
- Frequent overload trips = Check load or thermal setting

=======================================================
SECTION 6: PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
=======================================================
Daily:
- Check motor temperature (should be <100°C)
- Listen for unusual noise
- Check vibration level visually

Weekly:
- Check lubrication (bearing temperature <70°C)
- Inspect cooling fan operation
- Check motor mounting bolts

Monthly:
- Measure vibration with analyzer
- Check insulation resistance
- Inspect electrical connections

Every 6 months:
- Full bearing inspection
- Lubrication replacement
- Alignment check
- Thermal imaging

Annual:
- Full overhaul
- Bearing replacement if >10,000 hours
- Winding inspection

=======================================================
SECTION 7: SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
=======================================================
MANDATORY BEFORE ANY MAINTENANCE:
1. Switch off main isolator
2. Apply personal lockout device
3. Apply red DANGER tag
4. Test with voltmeter - verify dead
5. Allow 5 minutes for residual charge to dissipate

PPE REQUIRED:
- Safety glasses (EN166)
- Insulated gloves (Class 0 minimum)
- Safety boots (EN20345)
- Hard hat when working under equipment
- Ear protection if noise >85dB

NEVER:
- Work on live equipment
- Bypass thermal protection
- Operate with cooling fan removed
- Exceed rated load by more than 10%
