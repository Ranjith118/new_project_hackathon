BLAST FURNACE FAN - MAINTENANCE MANUAL
Siemens Industrial Fan Type: SIF-BF 2800
Rated Power: 500 kW | Speed: 980 RPM | Voltage: 6.6 kV
Application: Blast Furnace Hot Blast System - Steel Manufacturing Plant

=======================================================
SECTION 1: NORMAL OPERATING PARAMETERS
=======================================================
- Temperature (bearing housing): Max 85°C, Normal 40-65°C
- Temperature (motor winding): Max 130°C, Normal 70-90°C
- Vibration (fan side bearing): Max 3.5 mm/s, Normal 0.5-1.8 mm/s
- Vibration (motor side bearing): Max 3.0 mm/s, Normal 0.5-1.5 mm/s
- Current: Max 55A, Normal 38-45A
- Pressure (discharge): Normal 180-220 mbar
- Flow rate: Normal 85,000 - 95,000 Nm3/h
- RPM: 940-1020 RPM normal operating range
- Inlet temperature: Max 250°C (hot blast), Normal 180-220°C
- Outlet pressure: 200-250 mbar

CRITICAL ALARM THRESHOLDS:
- Bearing temperature > 90°C: STOP immediately
- Vibration > 4.5 mm/s: STOP immediately
- Current > 58A: REDUCE load
- Flow rate drop > 20%: CHECK for blockage

=======================================================
SECTION 2: BEARING MAINTENANCE
=======================================================
Bearing Type (Fan Side): Spherical Roller Bearing 22328 CC/W33
Bearing Type (Motor Side): Deep Groove Ball Bearing 6320
Lubrication: Mobil SHC Polyrex EP2 high-temperature grease
Regreasing interval: Every 1500 operating hours
Bearing replacement interval: Every 25,000 hours or on condition

BEARING REPLACEMENT PROCEDURE - FAN SIDE:
Step 1: Stop fan - Apply Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) on main 6.6kV isolator
Step 2: Verify dead with high-voltage tester - MANDATORY
Step 3: Allow bearing to cool to below 50°C (minimum 2 hours after shutdown)
Step 4: Release fan duct pressure - open manual bleed valve
Step 5: Disconnect flexible coupling between fan and motor
Step 6: Remove fan shaft bearing cover using 30mm socket
Step 7: Use hydraulic puller SKF TMFN 130 to extract bearing from shaft
Step 8: Clean shaft journal with emery cloth grade 240
Step 9: Check shaft diameter - must be 140mm +0.0/-0.03mm tolerance
Step 10: Heat new bearing to 100°C using induction heater - DO NOT exceed 120°C
Step 11: Quickly press heated bearing onto shaft using bearing mounting tool
Step 12: Apply 45g of Mobil SHC Polyrex EP2 grease (50% fill of housing cavity)
Step 13: Reinstall bearing cover - torque bolts to 120 Nm in star pattern
Step 14: Reconnect flexible coupling - check alignment with laser tool
Step 15: Run fan unloaded for 30 minutes - monitor bearing temperature every 5 minutes
Step 16: Gradually increase to full load over 2 hours

SPARE PARTS FOR BEARING REPLACEMENT:
- Bearing 22328 CC/W33 (Part No: BFF-001) x1 - fan side
- Bearing 6320 (Part No: BFM-002) x1 - motor side
- Grease Mobil SHC Polyrex EP2 - 1kg (Part No: GR-BF-001)
- Bearing cover gasket (Part No: BCG-BF-001) x2
- Flexible coupling insert (Part No: FC-BF-004) x1 - inspect, replace if worn
- O-ring set (Part No: OR-BF-001) x1 set

=======================================================
SECTION 3: IMPELLER AND ROTOR MAINTENANCE
=======================================================
Impeller Type: Backward curved, 12 blades
Impeller Diameter: 2800 mm
Material: Heat-resistant steel EN 1.4841

IMPELLER INSPECTION INTERVALS:
- Visual inspection: Every 3 months
- Dimensional check: Every 6 months
- Dynamic balancing: Every 12 months or after blade repair

IMPELLER INSPECTION PROCEDURE:
Step 1: Lockout/Tagout - all energy sources
Step 2: Open fan inspection door
Step 3: Inspect each blade for:
   - Erosion wear (acceptable limit: max 5mm depth)
   - Cracks (ZERO tolerance - replace blade immediately)
   - Build-up deposits (clean if > 3mm thick)
   - Blade tip clearance: must be 8-12mm
Step 4: Check hub welds for cracks using dye penetrant testing
Step 5: Measure blade thickness - replace if below 8mm

BLADE EROSION CAUSES:
- Dust particles in blast air - check inlet filters
- High velocity impact from debris
- Chemical corrosion from sulfur compounds
- Thermal fatigue from temperature cycling

=======================================================
SECTION 4: COOLING SYSTEM MAINTENANCE
=======================================================
The BFF fan uses water cooling for the motor and air cooling for the fan shaft bearings.

WATER COOLING SYSTEM (MOTOR):
- Coolant: Distilled water + 30% glycol
- Flow rate: Min 15 L/min, Normal 20-25 L/min
- Inlet temperature: Max 35°C, Normal 20-28°C
- Outlet temperature: Max 55°C, Normal 40-50°C
- Pressure: 2.5-3.5 bar

COOLING SYSTEM CHECKS:
Step 1: Check coolant flow rate - use ultrasonic flow meter
Step 2: Check coolant quality - pH must be 7.5-9.0
Step 3: Inspect heat exchanger fins - clean if fouled
Step 4: Check pump impeller for wear
Step 5: Inspect all hoses for cracks or hardening

OVERHEATING CAUSES:
1. Coolant flow reduced - check pump and valves
2. Heat exchanger fouled - clean with descaling agent
3. Air in coolant circuit - bleed and refill
4. Coolant contamination - drain, flush, refill

=======================================================
SECTION 5: VIBRATION DIAGNOSIS AND BALANCING
=======================================================
VIBRATION LIMITS:
- 0.0 - 1.8 mm/s: Normal - no action
- 1.9 - 2.5 mm/s: Monitor - check monthly
- 2.6 - 3.5 mm/s: WARNING - inspect within 7 days
- 3.6 - 4.5 mm/s: ALERT - inspect within 24 hours
- > 4.5 mm/s: STOP - shut down immediately

VIBRATION DIAGNOSIS:
1x RPM (16.3 Hz) dominant vibration: IMBALANCE
   -> Check for blade deposits or erosion
   -> Dynamic balance the impeller
   -> Check for loose blade bolts

2x RPM vibration: MISALIGNMENT
   -> Check flexible coupling condition
   -> Realign fan-motor coupling with laser tool
   -> Check foundation bolts (torque: 350 Nm)

High frequency (>100 Hz) vibration: BEARING DAMAGE
   -> Measure bearing temperature
   -> Check lubrication quality
   -> Replace bearing if vibration increasing

Broadband high frequency: FLOW TURBULENCE
   -> Check inlet duct for obstructions
   -> Verify damper position
   -> Check blade tip clearance

ON-SITE DYNAMIC BALANCING PROCEDURE:
Step 1: Run fan at rated speed, measure vibration baseline
Step 2: Attach trial weight (500g) at 0° position on impeller
Step 3: Run again, record vibration amplitude and phase
Step 4: Calculate correction weight and position using balance calculator
Step 5: Attach correction weight - weld or bolt to impeller hub
Step 6: Verify vibration reduced below 1.8 mm/s

=======================================================
SECTION 6: ELECTRICAL MAINTENANCE
=======================================================
Motor Type: Siemens 1LE1 Series
Voltage: 6.6 kV (HIGH VOLTAGE - only licensed HV electricians)
Insulation class: H (180°C)

ELECTRICAL CHECKS (6-MONTHLY):
- Insulation resistance: Min 100 MΩ at 5kV test voltage
- Winding resistance: Compare to factory values ±5%
- Stator temperature sensors: Verify calibration
- Bearing temperature sensors: Verify calibration ±2°C
- Speed sensor: Verify output signal

SOFT STARTER MAINTENANCE:
- Clean cooling fans every 3 months
- Check thyristor connections annually
- Verify ramp-up time setting: 45 seconds
- Verify ramp-down time setting: 60 seconds

MOTOR PROTECTION SETTINGS:
- Overload relay: 52A (115% of FLC)
- Locked rotor protection: 2.5 seconds
- Under-voltage protection: 5.5 kV
- Differential protection: 15% differential current
- Bearing temperature trip: 90°C
- Winding temperature trip: 130°C

=======================================================
SECTION 7: PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
=======================================================
Daily:
- Check bearing temperatures (should be <80°C)
- Check vibration level on HMI
- Check current reading (should be 38-45A)
- Listen for unusual noise at inspection hatch
- Verify flow rate on process display (85,000-95,000 Nm3/h)

Weekly:
- Check grease nipple condition
- Inspect flexible coupling visually
- Check inlet filter differential pressure
- Verify cooling water flow and temperature
- Log all parameters in maintenance register

Monthly:
- Measure vibration with portable analyzer
- Check coupling alignment (acceptable: <0.1mm offset)
- Inspect inlet ductwork for leaks
- Check impeller through inspection door
- Test emergency stop function
- Check all bolts for tightness

Every 3 months:
- Full impeller visual inspection
- Clean inlet filter elements
- Check heat exchanger condition
- Inspect flexible coupling insert
- Check motor cable connections
- Lubrication check and top-up

Every 6 months:
- Full electrical testing
- Dynamic balance check
- Coolant quality test and change if required
- Vibration spectrum analysis
- Bearing condition assessment (ultrasound)

Annual:
- Full overhaul - bearings, seals, coupling
- Impeller non-destructive testing
- Motor winding inspection
- Foundation inspection
- Performance test - flow rate vs pressure curve

=======================================================
SECTION 8: FAULT DIAGNOSIS TABLE
=======================================================
FAULT: High bearing temperature (>85°C)
CAUSE 1: Insufficient grease -> Action: Apply 45g Mobil SHC Polyrex EP2
CAUSE 2: Wrong grease type -> Action: Flush and repack with correct grease
CAUSE 3: Bearing overloaded -> Action: Check alignment and balance
CAUSE 4: Bearing damaged -> Action: Replace bearing BFF-001

FAULT: High vibration (>3.5 mm/s)
CAUSE 1: Imbalance -> Action: Clean blades, dynamic balance
CAUSE 2: Misalignment -> Action: Realign coupling, check foundation
CAUSE 3: Bearing failure -> Action: Ultrasound check, replace if necessary
CAUSE 4: Resonance -> Action: Check operating speed vs critical speed

FAULT: Low flow rate
CAUSE 1: Inlet filter blocked -> Action: Clean or replace filter elements
CAUSE 2: Damper not fully open -> Action: Check damper actuator and position
CAUSE 3: Blade erosion -> Action: Inspect blades, repair or replace
CAUSE 4: Duct leakage -> Action: Inspect and seal duct joints

FAULT: High motor current (>55A)
CAUSE 1: System resistance too high -> Action: Check damper and duct
CAUSE 2: Fan overloaded -> Action: Reduce blast volume via damper
CAUSE 3: Bearing friction -> Action: Check lubrication, inspect bearings
CAUSE 4: Electrical fault -> Action: Check motor windings and connections

=======================================================
SECTION 9: SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
=======================================================
MANDATORY BEFORE ANY MAINTENANCE:
1. Notify blast furnace control room - production impact
2. Confirm furnace in safe holding state
3. Switch off 6.6kV main isolator - HV AUTHORIZED PERSON ONLY
4. Apply personal lockout - EVERY person working must lock individually
5. Apply DANGER tag on isolator
6. Test with HV tester (6.6kV) - verify dead
7. Wait 10 minutes for residual charge to dissipate
8. Apply earthing clamps to all three phases
9. Bleed inlet duct pressure to zero
10. Allow hot parts to cool to safe temperature before touching

SPECIFIC HAZARDS FOR BLAST FURNACE FAN:
- HIGH VOLTAGE 6.6kV: Fatal if contacted - HV license required
- HOT SURFACES: Inlet duct reaches 250°C - burns risk
- NOISE LEVEL: >105 dB during operation - mandatory ear protection
- CONFINED SPACE: Fan housing is classified confined space
- DUST: Blast furnace dust contains iron oxide - respiratory protection
- CO GAS: Possible CO leakage from blast furnace - CO detector mandatory

PPE REQUIRED:
- Safety glasses (EN166)
- HV insulated gloves (Class 4 - 36kV rated)
- Safety boots steel toe (EN20345)
- Hard hat (EN397)
- Ear protection (EN352) - Class 4 minimum
- Dust mask (FFP3) when inside fan housing
- CO gas detector when working near ductwork

CONFINED SPACE ENTRY:
- Permit to work required
- Continuous gas monitoring
- Minimum 2 persons - one standby outside
- Emergency rescue plan in place
- Communication maintained throughout

EMERGENCY CONTACTS:

- Maintenance Emergency: Extension 102
- Medical: Extension 999
- Fire: Extension 101
